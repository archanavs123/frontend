The northernmost district of Kerala, Kasaragod is renowned for its hills, forts and water bodies. The coir and handloom industry here are extremely famous around the world. The place is also popular for its ancient temples and rich biodiversity. It is an important border district as well that regulates trade with a lot of neighbouring States. Legends regarding the district greet you at every turn on a visit here. A trip here is incomplete without visiting Bekal Fort, the most majestic and well maintained fort in South India

Kasargod, the land of seven languages and several cultures. The place of harmonious coexistence of Hindu, Muslim, Christian religions. Kasaragod is known as the Land of Gods, Forts, Rivers, Beautiful hills and lengthy sandy beaches. visit to these places evoke enthralling moments to the visitors

Bekal Fort beach, which is situated about a kilometre from Bekal, provides a breathtaking panoramic view of the fort. The shallow waters of the beach have made it a perfect picnic spot for locals and tourists alike. The beach boasts of various recreational spots, including a well preserved garden and a children's park. The walkway built along the coastline offers visitors a chance to explore the coast in a safe and enjoyable way.

Bekal fort (120 23′ N and 750 02′ E), a coastal fort lying 16 km south-east of Kasargod situated on the backdrop of Arabian seashore of Pallikkara village in the Kasargod district (Kerala). It is one of the best preserved forts in Kerala.Bekal, a serene coastal town in Kerala, is a treasure trove of natural beauty and historical significance. With its pristine beaches, tranquil backwaters, and majestic forts, Bekal offers a unique blend of relaxation and adventure.


The Govinda Pai Memorial is a museum and research center located in the town of Manjeshwar in Kasaragod. It is dedicated to the life and works of Govinda Pai, a famous Indian poet and scholar who lived in the early 20th century. Awards and legacy. In 1949, the then Madras Government conferred on him the Rashtrakavi award. He was the president of Kannada Sahitya Sammelana at Bombay in 1951. On his 125th birth anniversary, a national award was instituted in his name and his old house at Manjeshwar is mooted to be a national monument.Manjeshwara Govinda Pai, hailing from Manjeshwara, Kasargod, was the first Kannada poet to be conferred the title of Rashtrakavi by the Madras government in 1949. On this occasion, the Father of the Nation Mahatma Gandhi honored Maithili Sharan Gupt with the title of National Poet. Since then Maithili Sharan Gupt has been recognized as a national poet. He was given the most recognized Hindi award for Saket by Hindi Sahitya Sammelan in 1937. In 1954 he was given Padma Bhushan.
